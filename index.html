Data Science Projects


Geospatial Analysis of Gentrification on NYC

A geo-spatial analysis of social and economical variables that have been linked to the process of gentrification on NYC. Done as final project for GIS Spatial Analysis using QGIS, R, d3 and CartoDb. Written report located here.

MoMA Through Time

Winner of MoMA's Art Datathon event. We utilized the museum's collection data to explore the most exhibited artists and genres through time, revealing interesting patterns on their selection.


Summer (Neural) Networks '16

Several small projects and tutorials performed during the summer on Neural Networks and their creative applications. Implementation both on numpy and theano, with the input corpus a set of Bukowski's poems.

CULPA Sentiment

Sentiment analysis and visualization of Columbia's academic course ratings. Won prize for best use of the API.

Data Apps



GeoTracking App

Built for Trinnacle Capital Management. Uses mobile geolocation data to fit a linear model based on user parameters and predict companies' revenue.


Process Monitor

Built for Kora Management LP. Monitors the status of several web-scraping processes running on AWS and provides drill-down details on them (running time, success rate, items collected).


Twitter Brand Monitor

Collects tweets related to a company and its products, performs sentiment analysis on it, and extracts user-ratings for them. The data is presented as time-series with drill-down options.

More About Me

Short Bio

I graduated as a B.Sc. in Economics from Monterrey Tech (ITESM) and worked at MSCI as a quantitative data analyst for 5 years, where I helped build and manage the Market Data team. After playing with R, Python and big data visualization tools, I decided to purse graduate studies on the subject. Right now I'm going over the last semester of the M.S. in Data Science Program at Columbia, and have developed more practical experience during 2 internships here in NY: the first at Trinnacle Capital Management, and the second at Kora Management L.P.
